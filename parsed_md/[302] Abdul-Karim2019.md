## **Poly(propylene ether carbonate)-Based Di- and Tri-Block Copolymers: Synthesis and Chromatographic Characterization** 

Rubina Abdul-Karim Syed Ghulam Musharraf Muhammad Imran Malik* 

H.E.J. Research Institute of Chemistry, International Centre for Chemical and Biological Sciences (ICCBS), University of Karachi, Karachi 75270, Pakistan 

Received October 30, 2018 / Revised February 12, 2019 / Accepted February 13, 2019 

Abstract: In this study, ring-opening polymerization (ROP) of propylene carbonate (PC) is conducted for synthesis of novel amphiphilic di- and tri-block copolymers containing poly(ethylene oxide) as hydrophilic with poly(propylene ether-carbonate (PPEC) as a hydrophobic block. A series of di- and tri-block copolymers is synthesized, by using macro-initiators [polyethylene glycol monomethyl ethers and poly(ethylene glycol)s] of different molar masses, while systematically varying  monomer to macro-initiator ratio. The synthesized block copolymers are characterized by size exclusion chromatography, for augmentation in the molar mass, NMR for average chemical composition while liquid chromatography at critical conditions, is used for comparison of individual block lengths alongside purity of block copolymers, with regard to presence of homopolymers of both types. Chromatographic critical conditions of the polymer synthesized by ROP of PC, PPEC, are reported for the first time. Established chromatographic critical conditions of PEG and PPEC, successfully tracked presence of homopolymers of both types, with fair comparison of individual block lengths of the block copolymers synthesized by the same macroinitiator. 

Keywords: biodegradable polymers, poly(propylene ether-carbonate), amphiphilic block copolymers, liquid chromatography at critical conditions, chromatographic critical conditions of poly(propylene ether-carbonate). 

## **1. Introduction** 

Covalently joined hydrophilic and hydrophobic polymeric segments generate species having completely different properties compared to their parent materials and are termed as amphiphilic block copolymers. Amphiphilic block copolymers selfassemble in selective solvent for one of the segment to form core-shell type micellar structures. Polymeric micelles have attracted considerable attention lately due to their applications in various fields of science such as surface coating, detergents, oil recovery, nanotechnology, and drug delivery etc.[1,2] These are nano-sized vesicles that have ability to encapsulate hydrophobic drugs in the aqueous environment of body. Sustained and continuous delivery of the drug to the targeted site till the next planned dose is one of the major challenges of modern day medical science. 

Poly(ethylene oxide) (PEO) is the most widely employed polymer as hydrophilic segment for designing biodegradable amphiphilic block copolymers due to its excellent biocompatibility, water solubility and non-toxicity.[3,4] However, the selection of hydrophobic segment from a fairly long list such as polyacrylates, polyether, polyesters, and polycarbonates etc depends upon the intended application. Amphiphilic block copolymers based on polycarbonates have been synthesized by ring-opening polymerization (ROP) of six-membered cyclic carbonates mainly trimethylene carbonate.[8,9] However, ROP of five-membered cyclic carbonates for synthesis of amphiphilic block copolymers is not given much consideration in literature. ## **2. HPLC of polymers** 

Chromatographic behaviour of polymers depends upon sizepore ratio and interaction parameter c of the repeat unit with the stationary phase. The interaction parameter c is measured in inverse length (nm[-1] ). Its value depends strongly on mobile phase composition (for a given polymer-sorbent system) and is independent of pore diameter, interstitial volume, pore volume and degree of polymerization. Based on the so called interaction parameter c, polymer chromatography can be sub-divided into three distinct modes of separation namely, SEC, interaction chromatography (IC) and liquid chromatography at critical condition (LCCC). The value of interaction parameter c is negative in SEC, positive in IC and equals to zero in case of LCCC.[18-21] 

SEC separates polymers according to their hydrodynamic volume in dilute solution. It is entropy controlled process and allows separation of polymers in order of decreasing hydrodynamic volume. The separation in SEC regime is realized before the void volume of the column within the limits of the pore volume. The hydrodynamic volume can then be correlated to the molar mass of the polymer standards used for calibration of the system. The exact correlation is only possible if polymer standards of same polymer having same architecture are used. Therefore, SEC can only provide molar mass relative to the used standards. Furthermore, discrimination of polymers with regard to chemical composition is not possible by SEC. However, SEC is a reasonable first test for any newly synthesized polymer with regard to augmentation in hydrodynamic size that is an indication of increase in the molar mass of the polymers.[22-24] 

The presence of polymer segments of different chemical nature in block copolymers makes situation gloomier. The interaction parameters cA and cB of individual structural units (A and B) may have different values. SEC separation mode will be opera- 

tive if both cA and cB are negative. However, the hydrodynamic volumes of both structural units might be different that further complicates the situation. Nonetheless, SEC can be used as a first test for augmentation in the hydrodynamic size of the block copolymers. 

Positive values of interaction parameters cA and cB of individual structural units (A and B) is of no practical use in analysis of block copolymers since it can result in mess of peaks that are impossible to interpret. However, IC can be employed for oligomeric separation of homopolymers and functional polymers. Solvent and temperature gradients can be used for analysis of complex polymers, however, the approach is based on the principle of altering the interaction strength of the repeat unit with the stationary phase during the run.[18,19,25,26] 

Another very important mode of liquid chromatography of polymers exists at the intersection of two extreme modes of LC of polymers, where the value of interaction parameter c equals zero. These conditions are termed as LCCC of that particular polymer in a given solvent/sorbent system. The establishment of LCCC results in molar mass independent elution of homopolymers of that particular type near the void volume of the column.[18,19] The situation is particularly useful while analyzing block copolymers[10,27-33] or functional polymers.[30,34-36] The analysis can provide information with regard to molar mass distribution of noncritical block along with presence or absence of unwanted critical homopolymers in the sample. LCCC has been used for analysis of block copolymers where noncritical block elutes either in exclusion[10,27-29] or interaction regime.[30-33] 

LCCC of hydrophilic block can be realized on reversed phase (RP) column in a highly polar mobile phase allowing hydrophobic block to elute in IC mode. On the same column, critical conditions of hydrophobic block can be realized in a non-polar mobile phase allowing exclusion of hydrophilic block. Similarly, critical conditions of hydrophilic block can be realized in a polar mobile phase on normal phase (NP) column allowing exclusion of hydrophobic block. LCCC of hydrophobic block may be possible on NP column in a mobile phase of low polarity allowing adsorption of hydrophilic segment.

Peculiar slow degradation rate and non-acid degradation products of PPEC makes them fascinating candidates for drug delivery applications. ## **5. Conclusions** 

Amphiphilic di- and tri-block copolymers containing PPEC as a hydrophobic block and PEO as hydrophilic part are synthesized for the first time. The synthesized novel amphiphilic block copolymers are analyzed by SEC, NMR and LCCC. LCCC of the polymer synthesized by ROP of PC, PPEC, are reported. The statistical nature of ether and carbonate units in the hydrophobic segment as revealed by NMR analysis is endorsed by chromatography. Block copolymer analysis at critical conditions allows for determination of free PEO and/or PPEC in the samples. Interactive block at critical conditions of PEG have major effect on the elution of block copolymer compared to homopolymers of excluded block. Furthermore, the products with lower initial monomer content have considerable amounts of unreacted macro-initiator in the samples. However, all macro-initiator reacted while using higher content of PC in the reaction mixture, making a part of block copolymer. The meticulous analysis protocols confirmed the successful synthesis of the block copolymers and allowed for comparison of relative length of both blocks. The synthesized biodegradable block copolymers can open new possibilities for modern drug delivery systems due to slow degradation rate and non-acid biodegradation products of polycarbonates.
